## The 6-Level Governance Hierarchy

Mohammed OS uses a 6-level governance hierarchy. Each level has different authority, change frequency, and approval requirements.

```
Level 1: CONSTITUTION        (Founder only — rare changes)
Level 2: AMENDMENTS          (Founder only — occasional)
Level 3: FRAMEWORKS          (Founder or delegated — periodic)
Level 4: STANDARDS           (Founder or delegated — regular)
Level 5: PLAYBOOKS           (Founder or delegated — frequent)
Level 6: KNOWLEDGE           (All contributors — continuous)
```

### Level 1: Constitution

The Constitution is the root document. It defines authority, decision-making, immutable principles, and amendment processes. It is small (~1,500 words) and permanent.

**Document:** [CONSTITUTION-001-v2.md](CONSTITUTION-001-v2.md)

### Level 2: Constitutional Amendments

Amendments modify the Constitution. They require Founder approval and follow an increasing difficulty curve based on organizational maturity.

**None yet.** The first amendment will be numbered Amendment-001.

### Level 3: Frameworks

Frameworks define how Mohammed OS operates in specific domains. They implement constitutional mandates with detailed processes, criteria, and workflows.

| Document | Domain | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| [FRAMEWORK-001](FRAMEWORK-001-Strategic-Decision.md) | Strategic Decisions | 22-stage lifecycle, 6 decision gates, confidence scoring |
| [FRAMEWORK-002](FRAMEWORK-002-BUILD.md) | BUILD Phases | Structure, criteria, portfolio rules, roadmap |
| [FRAMEWORK-003](FRAMEWORK-003-Competitive-Intelligence.md) | Competitive Intelligence | Mandatory research before any strategic investment |

### Level 4: Standards

Standards define technical and quality requirements. They are prescriptive — work must meet these standards.

| Document | Domain | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| [STANDARD-001](STANDARD-001-Engineering.md) | Engineering | Tech stack, code standards, database, security, testing |

### Level 5: Playbooks

Playbooks are step-by-step execution guides. They tell you how to do something, not just what the standards are.

| Document | Domain | Description |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| [PLAYBOOK-001](PLAYBOOK-001-Product-Development.md) | Product Development | How to execute a BUILD phase from design through deployment |

### Level 6: Knowledge

Knowledge is the continuously updated output of Mohammed OS operations: research reports, decision records, learning records, and operational data.

| Knowledge Type | Examples |
|----------------|----------|
| Research Reports | PROJECT-001 (Competitive Intelligence), IDEA-002 (KnowOps) |
| Decision Records | BUILD approvals, strategic decisions |
| Learning Records | Post-BUILD reviews, outcome assessments |
| Operational Data | Usage metrics, performance data, error logs |

## Change Control

### Who can change what?

| Document Type | Can Be Changed By | Approval Required |
|--------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Constitution | No one (immutable principles) / Founder (governable principles) | Founder |
| Amendments | Founder | Founder |
| Frameworks | Executive Chief of Staff | Founder notification |
| Standards | Atlas | Founder or Executive Chief of Staff |
| Playbooks | Chief of Staff | Executive Chief of Staff |
| Knowledge | All contributors | Peer review (for research reports) |

### What cannot be changed?

- Principles 1–4 of the Constitution (Immutable Principles)
- The 6-level hierarchy itself
- The requirement that lower levels cannot contradict higher levels
- The Competitive Intelligence Requirement (Section 5)
