# FRAMEWORK-003: Competitive Intelligence Framework
## Strategic Landscape Research Before Investment

**Level:** 3 — Framework
**Governed By:** CONSTITUTION-001, Article II, Section 5
**Changed By:** Founder or Executive Chief of Staff
**Last Updated:** July 17, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This Framework implements the constitutional requirement that **no strategic investment decision receives Founder approval without completed Strategic Landscape Research**.

Every initiative must answer seven questions before advancing to Gate 2 (Investment Gate):
1. Does this already exist?
2. Who solves this today?
3. What are their strengths?
4. What are their weaknesses?
5. Should Mohammed OS BUILD, BUY, INTEGRATE, or IGNORE?
6. What is our sustainable differentiation?
7. What is the competitive risk if we do nothing?

## 2. When Competitive Intelligence Is Required

| Decision Type | Intelligence Required | Minimum Depth |
|--------------|----------------------|---------------|
| New capability (BUILD) | **Mandatory** | Full landscape analysis |
| External integration | **Mandatory** | Vendor + alternative analysis |
| Capability purchase | **Mandatory** | Market + pricing analysis |
| Architecture change | **Mandatory** | Technical landscape analysis |
| Minor enhancement | Recommended | Lightweight scan |
| Bug fix / maintenance | Not required | — |

## 3. Research Process

### Step 1: Define the Question (1 day)
- What capability is being considered?
- What problem does it solve?
- Who is the primary user?
- What is the success metric?

### Step 2: Identify Competitors and Alternatives (2–3 days)
- Direct competitors (same capability, same market)
- Indirect competitors (same capability, different market)
- Adjacent solutions (different capability, same problem)
- DIY/internal solutions (customers building their own)
- Academic/research solutions (not yet commercialized)

### Step 3: Analyze Each Alternative (2–3 days)
For each identified alternative, document:
- Company / product name
- What they do
- Who they serve
- Key strengths
- Key weaknesses
- Pricing (if available)
- Market position
- Recent changes (funding, product, leadership)

### Step 4: Synthesize and Recommend (1 day)
- Summary of landscape
- Gap analysis (what is missing in the market)
- BUILD / BUY / INTEGRATE / IGNORE recommendation
- Sustainable differentiation argument (if BUILD)
- Risk assessment (if IGNORE)

### Step 5: Document and Attach (ongoing)
- Attach Competitive Intelligence Report to Gate 1 package
- Store in organizational memory
- Update when significant market changes occur

## 4. Research Depth Levels

| Level | Name | Scope | Time | When Used |
|-------|------|-------|------|-----------|
| **L1** | Lightweight Scan | 3–5 alternatives, brief analysis | 1–2 days | Minor enhancements, low-risk decisions |
| **L2** | Standard Analysis | 5–10 alternatives, detailed analysis | 3–5 days | Most BUILD phase proposals |
| **L3** | Deep Landscape | 10+ alternatives, full market analysis | 5–10 days | Major capabilities, new categories |
| **L4** | Comprehensive Study | Full market + academic + patent research | 10+ days | Strategic pivots, category creation |

## 5. BUILD / BUY / INTEGRATE / IGNORE Decision Matrix

| Factor | BUILD | BUY | INTEGRATE | IGNORE |
|--------|-------|-----|-----------|--------|
| **When to choose** | Core IP, no good alternative, differentiation critical | Commodity, solved problem, faster time-to-value | Complementary, best-in-class, API available | Not needed, low priority, risk > reward |
| **Examples for Mohammed OS** | Workspace isolation, organizational memory, Founder governance | AI model inference, compliance tooling, search | Productivity suites (M365/GSuite), CRM (Salesforce), workflow (n8n) | Video conferencing, accounting, email delivery |
| **Risk** | High (build wrong thing) | Medium (vendor lock-in) | Low-Medium (dependency) | Low (missed opportunity) |
| **Time to value** | Longest | Shortest | Medium | N/A |

## 6. Quality Standards

A Competitive Intelligence Report meets quality standards when:
- [ ] At least 3 alternatives were identified and analyzed
- [ ] Each alternative has documented strengths and weaknesses
- [ ] The recommendation (BUILD/BUY/INTEGRATE/IGNORE) is explicit
- [ ] Sustainable differentiation is argued (if BUILD)
- [ ] Risk of doing nothing is assessed
- [ ] Research is dated and signed by the researching officer
- [ ] Sources are documented (not "I heard that…")

## 7. Update Requirements

Competitive intelligence is not static. Updates are required when:
- A tracked competitor makes a significant move (funding, product, acquisition)
- 90 days have passed since the last update for active initiatives
- The Founder requests a refresh
- A new competitor enters the market

## 8. Integration with Strategic Decision Framework

The Competitive Intelligence Report is a **mandatory attachment** to the Gate 1 (Evaluation Gate) package. It is reviewed alongside:
- The Research Report
- The Validated Hypothesis
- The Adversarial Analysis

Without a Competitive Intelligence Report, the initiative **cannot advance to Gate 2**.
