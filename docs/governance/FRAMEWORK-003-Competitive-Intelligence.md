# FRAMEWORK-003: Competitive Intelligence Framework
## Strategic Landscape Research Before Investment

**Level:** 3 — Framework
**Governed By:** CONSTITUTION-001, Article II, Section 5
**Changed By:** Founder or Executive Chief of Staff
**Last Updated:** July 17, 2026

---

## 1. Purpose

This Framework defines how Mohammed OS conducts competitive intelligence research before strategic investment decisions. It implements the constitutional mandate that **no strategic investment decision advances to Founder approval without completed Strategic Landscape Research.**

This requirement is constitutional. It cannot be waived.

## 2. When Competitive Intelligence Is Required

**Always required before:**
- Any BUILD phase approval (Gate 2)
- Any partnership or integration decision
- Any buy vs. build decision
- Any market entry decision

**Not required for:**
- Bug fixes and maintenance
- UI/UX improvements within approved designs
- Performance optimizations
- Operational decisions within delegated scope

## 3. The 5-Step Research Process

### Step 1: Define the Research Question

Every competitive intelligence effort starts with a clear question:

| Question Type | Example |
|---------------|---------|
| Capability mapping | "Who offers knowledge management for executive teams?" |
| Market sizing | "How big is the AI executive assistant market?" |
| Differentiation analysis | "What do existing solutions miss that we could provide?" |
| Integration assessment | "Who has APIs we could integrate with?" |
| Threat assessment | "Who could enter our space and compete?" |

### Step 2: Identify the Competitive Landscape

**Primary sources (always use):**
- Company websites and documentation
- Product demos and trials
- User reviews (G2, Capterra, TrustRadius)
- Pricing pages and public information

**Secondary sources (use when available):**
- Industry analyst reports (Gartner, Forrester, IDC)
- Academic research and papers
- News and press releases
- Social media and community discussions

**Minimum coverage:**
- At least 3 direct competitors (same category, same audience)
- At least 2 adjacent competitors (different category, overlapping audience)
- At least 1 potential entrant (large company that could expand into this space)

### Step 3: Evaluate Each Competitor

| Evaluation Dimension | Questions to Answer |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| **Capabilities** | What do they do? What don't they do? |
| **Strengths** | What are they best at? What do users love? |
| **Weaknesses** | What do users complain about? What's missing? |
| **Scale** | How many users? Revenue (if public)? Market presence? |
| **Pricing** | What do they charge? What's the pricing model? |
| **Architecture** | How is it built? What's the technical approach? |
| **Differentiation** | What makes them unique? What's their moat? |
| **Gaps** | What do they miss that Mohammed OS could provide? |

### Step 4: Synthesize Findings

**Required synthesis documents:**

1. **Competitive Landscape Map:** Visual representation of competitors positioned by capability and market presence
2. **Capability Gap Analysis:** Matrix of features/capabilities vs. competitors showing what's covered and what's missing
3. **BUILD/BUY/INTEGRATE/IGNORE Recommendation:** For each capability area, clear recommendation with rationale

### Step 5: Record and Present

**Deliverables:**
- Competitive Intelligence Report (follows standard template)
- Presentation to Founder (if Gate 2 decision)
- Update to Knowledge base for future reference

## 4. Research Depth Levels

| Level | Scope | Time Budget | When to Use |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------|
| **L1: Quick Scan** | 3–5 competitors, basic capabilities | 2–4 hours | Early exploration, fast-track decisions |
| **L2: Standard** | 5–10 competitors, full evaluation | 1–2 days | Regular BUILD phase approval |
| **L3: Deep** | 10–20 competitors, detailed analysis | 3–5 days | Major strategic decisions, new domains |
| **L4: Comprehensive** | 20+ competitors, market sizing, trend analysis | 1–2 weeks | Market entry, constitutional-level decisions |

**Default for BUILD phase approval: L2 (Standard)**

## 5. BUILD/BUY/INTEGRATE/IGNORE Decision Matrix

| Recommendation | When to Apply | Example |
|---------------|---------------|---------|
| **BUILD** | No adequate solution exists, strategic differentiator, core to mission | Mohammed OS workspace architecture |
| **BUY** | Excellent solution exists, not strategic differentiator, faster/cheaper to buy | Infrastructure (hosting, monitoring) |
| **INTEGRATE** | Good solution exists, complements our capabilities, API available | External AI models, payment processing |
| **IGNORE** | Adequate solutions exist, not aligned with mission, opportunity cost too high | Generic CRM features, social media management |

## 6. Quality Standards

**Every Competitive Intelligence Report must:**
- [ ] Cover minimum competitor count (3 direct, 2 adjacent, 1 potential)
- [ ] Include verified data (not speculation)
- [ ] Cite sources for all claims
- [ ] Include adversarial analysis (what could we be wrong about?)
- [ ] Provide clear BUILD/BUY/INTEGRATE/IGNORE recommendations
- [ ] Identify sustainable differentiation opportunities
- [ ] Be dated and versioned
- [ ] Be recorded in the Knowledge base

## 7. Post-Decision Tracking

After a strategic decision is made:

- Competitive landscape is re-scanned every 90 days
- New entrants are flagged immediately
- Significant competitor changes trigger re-evaluation
- BUILD/BUY/INTEGRATE recommendations are reviewed quarterly
